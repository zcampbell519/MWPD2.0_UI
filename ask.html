Hundreds of Uses

It’s logs and posts, timber frames, trusses and 2x4s, and much, much more; wood products sold in Montana include a long list of uses for building interiors as well as exteriors and landscaping. Montana boasts one of the few U.S. manufacturers of innovative, engineered wood that is part of the rapidly expanding mass timber trend in building construction.

Dozens of small mills and wood working shops turn out furniture and other unique pieces for homeowners. You can find signs, picture frames, musical instruments, bows, gunstocks, and watercraft—all made in Montana.

Don’t forget Montana-made products for your pellet stove or fireplace. Various manufacturers in the state offer firelogs, pellets or chips.

Slower-growing, Tighter Grain, Stronger Wood

Lumber milled from Montana’s renewable softwoods tends to be stronger than softwood lumber from the south and coastal Pacific northwest. Our long Montana winters keep the growing season short, which means slower-growing trees. The slower growth creates a tighter grain. This tighter grain has both increased strength and visual appeal

Montana Forests and Trees

Forests cover about one-fourth of Montana’s land area. Ponderosa and lodgepole pine, Douglas fir, larch, grand fir, spruce, western hemlock and western red cedar grow west of the Continental Divide. Timber stands of primarily ponderosa pine are found on the drier east side of the state. Deciduous native species include box elder, paper birch, green ash, balsam poplar, cottonwood, aspen, buckthorn, willow and elm.

What if your building supply store doesn’t carry Montana Wood?

The remaining 7 large mills in the state are wholesale operations. The sale of milled wood goes through a broker who can choose any source. Ask your supplier or your contractor to offer Montana-grown lumber. When you choose Montana wood, you support healthy forests and defend our clean air and water.
